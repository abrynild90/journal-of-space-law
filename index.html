The World's Oldest Space Law Journal

Founded in 1973 by Professor Stephen Gorove, the Journal of Space Law is the oldest and most respected scholarly publication dedicated exclusively to legal issues in outer space.

Published by the University of Mississippi's Center for Air and Space Law, the Journal covers international treaties, commercial space activities, national space legislation, and the emerging legal challenges of the new space age.

Today, under the leadership of Executive Director and Professor Michelle Hanlon, the Journal continues to serve as the premier forum for space law scholarship. Learn more →

About the Journal

The Center for Air and Space Law and the University of Mississippi School of Law have been at the forefront of space law since the 1970s. We're proud to publish the Journal of Space Law, our Center's flagship publication and a world-renowned journal on outer space.

Founded by Professor Stephen Gorove, our Journal has featured articles from pioneers in space law including: Eilene Galloway, Myres McDougal, Bin Cheng, Francis Lyall, and countless others.

Today, under the leadership of the Center's Executive Director and Professor Michelle Hanlon, we continue to provide cutting-edge research on space law for the international community.
